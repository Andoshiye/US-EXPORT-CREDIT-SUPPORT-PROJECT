# US-EXPORT-CREDIT-SUPPORT-PROJECT

# Understanding U.S. Export Credit Support Through Data Analysis

## Project Overview

This project explores U.S. export credit financing data to understand how government backed financing supports American exporters. The project combines **Python** for data cleaning, feature engineering, exploratory data analysis (EDA), and statistical insights with **Power BI** for building an interactive business intelligence dashboard.

The analysis uncovers patterns in financing across industries, exporters, geographic regions, financing programs, and lending institutions, while the dashboard enables stakeholders to explore key performance indicators and financing trends through interactive visualizations.

The objective is to identify who receives export financing, where funding is concentrated, how effectively approved financing translates into actual export activity, and to provide decision makers with an intuitive dashboard for monitoring the export financing portfolio.

---

## Business Questions

This analysis answers the following questions:

* Who receives U.S. export credit support?
* Which industries receive the most financing?
* Which U.S. states and countries receive the highest support?
* Is financing concentrated among a few exporters?
* How has export financing changed over time?
* What percentage of approved financing is ultimately disbursed?
* What patterns exist among cancelled deals?
* Do brokered applications have higher approval rates?

The Power BI dashboard further explores:

* Executive portfolio performance and KPIs.
* Geographic distribution of financing.
* Industry and financing program performance.
* Exporter and lender analysis.
* Financing trends across fiscal years.

## Key Insights

* Aerospace and Defense received the largest share of export financing.
* Washington, Texas, and California accounted for the highest financing among U.S. states.
* Approximately 84.5% of approved financing was successfully disbursed.
* Insurance and Guarantee programs recorded the highest number of cancelled deals, although cancelled transactions represented only a small share of total financing value.
* Brokered applications showed a slightly higher approval rate than direct applications, but the difference was minimal.
